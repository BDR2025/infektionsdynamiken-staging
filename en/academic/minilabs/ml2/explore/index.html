Use this panel as a reading guide for the hidden phase in the SEIR wave.

ML2 / Explore

Make the hidden delay readable

SEIR becomes interesting when you stop looking only at the visible red wave and start reading what happens before it.

This Explore step is about understanding that the system can already be changing while infectiousness is not yet fully visible in the chart.

If you can describe why the visible infection peak comes later than the exposed build-up, then this Explore step has already done important work.

Read the three open controls before dragging them

In the academic variant you compare duration, latency and mitigation together.

The key reading task is to separate delay from damping. Latency shifts when infection becomes visible; mitigation changes how strongly the visible wave grows.

Duration D

What it changes

How long infectious activity stays effective after the hidden phase.

Watch for

Whether the visible wave stretches and keeps pressure longer.

Latency L

What it changes

How long exposure remains hidden before infectiousness becomes visible.

Watch for

Whether the yellow phase leads further and the red phase arrives later.

Mitigation

What it changes

How strongly contacts are reduced once transmission is happening.

Watch for

Whether the later visible wave also becomes flatter and less intense.

R0 and horizon stay fixed so that the delayed structure remains readable while you compare the open levers.

Use the KPI cards to compare hidden and visible peaks

The KPI cards help you read the delay as a relationship, not as a vague impression.

Peak E

How high the exposed phase gets before people become infectious.

Peak E day

When the hidden exposed phase reaches its maximum.

Peak I

How high the visible infectious wave becomes.

Peak I day

When the visible infectious peak actually arrives.

Good reading means: explain the gap. Say not only that the red wave moved, but whether the exposed phase moved first and how the delay between hidden and visible dynamics changed.

What you should be able to say after this

If this Explore worked, you now read SEIR as a model with a hidden phase, not just as a different-looking wave.

You should be able to explain the difference between a longer hidden phase, a longer infectious phase and a flatter later wave through mitigation.

When this reading feels stable, the next sensible step later is Continue in Learning Journey.
